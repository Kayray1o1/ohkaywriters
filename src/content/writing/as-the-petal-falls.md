"So, what is your impression of Alloria thus far? Compared to the world you say you grew up in?"

"It's weird," Grace said, and smiled at Philip.

Each time she smiled, his heartbeat accelerated. Her eyes always lit a fire in him.

"But Gwen would be so jealous if she knew I was here. I wonder if she's worried about me… It's been twenty-four hours, so she must have reported me missing by now."

"Who is Gwen?"

"My closest friend. She and I do everything together." Philip glanced back at Rowan. "Yes, I know what that feels like."

"You're not afraid of dying, are you?" Grace asked out of the blue. Philip frowned.

"What makes you say that?"

"Just the way you stormed in to kill the trolls… headfirst."

"Blade first. If I'd used my head, I'm fairly sure I'd be halfway eaten by now," Philip said, and smiled.

"What do you think is in those woods?"

"Nothing good — which is why my men will deal with it first thing upon my return."

"What creatures scare you… in Alloria?"

"Nothing much. Many creatures here are magical, but most aren't immune to the cold blade of steel. There is one thing I fear more than anything, though."

"A dragon?" she asked.

"Dragons are a myth here, believe it or not," Philip said. "What of your world?"

Grace nodded. "Go on then. You have to tell me."

"To satisfy your curiosity." Philip pulled out his chain necklace, revealing a long, sharp tooth. "This creature goes by no real name. It's a creature of the night that feasts on the flesh and blood of anything that moves near it. However — if one wears its tooth or claw, they're said to be invisible in its eyes." He took it off. "Here you go."

"Oh, no, I couldn't —"

"Why do you always deny my gifts when you know full well I'll insist until you accept?"

"I feel like you're giving me this because of Geneva. Not because of me."

"And what if you are one and the same?"

"I doubt that."

"Alright then. On that note: I, King Philip, hereby decree that I give this necklace to you, as Grace, and you may keep it as Grace. If you ever realise you are also Geneva, you may give it back — and then I'll give it to you as Geneva."

Grace scoffed softly and shook her head.

"Take it." He held it out. She accepted, and slipped it over her head.

She stared at the tooth. "I still feel like you're going to be disappointed."

"After everything… I doubt it," Philip said.

"What is Geneva to you? Really?"

Philip considered what to tell her. He didn't want to frighten her with the truth of their relationship, nor expect her to dive straight into it. He would start at the beginning.

"Geneva is someone very special to me. She and I have been through a great deal, and we both made a promise to keep fighting for each other until the end."

"Wow."

"What is it?"

"It's just… it sounds a little unreal."

"Why?" he asked. Grace only shook her head, and Philip could do nothing but guess at what was going on in hers.

"Thank you for the necklace. But if you ever want it back, you can ask."

"I won't. Not until you embrace your other name, and your other life."

"And what if that never happens?"

"Then you keep the necklace, and I'll find you your way home… if that's your wish."

"It is my wish," Grace said. "I'm worried about what's happening there — with my father, my brother… you know?" She spurred her horse onward, leaving Rowan to catch up with Philip.
