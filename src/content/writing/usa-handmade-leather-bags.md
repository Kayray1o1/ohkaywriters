## A Story to Tell: The History of Handmade Leather Bags

USA handmade leather bags have been around for centuries, which means many of them have stories to tell — stories of craftsmanship, art, and skill handed down for generations.

Tanning leather has existed for hundreds of years. In fact, it's one of the oldest professions still practised today. Leather was naturally used for all kinds of items: gloves, shoes, and armour, as well as weapons, bottles, and bucket bags.

Leather first came to America during colonial times, and by the Industrial Revolution the fur, hide, and skin trade had grown into a multi-billion-dollar industry. Today it remains a lucrative market, though quality varies widely — generally, the more expensive the item, the higher the quality. It's fair to say handmade leather bags have been around a very long time, and they aren't going anywhere soon.

## How Big Is the Handmade Leather Bag Market?

The global leather market is estimated at over $400 billion, with luxury leather goods accounting for roughly $66 billion of that. Within the USA alone, luxury leather goods represent billions more. These are big figures — and a clear sign of leather's staying power.
