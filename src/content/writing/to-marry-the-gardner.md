"Let me get this correct—"

Nika grinned at Hazel as she took a sip of her milkshake. They were sitting outside Cafe Petal & Bean, one of their favorite places to go whenever they took a break. Nika leaned back on her metallic chair. She spotted a bee flying past one of the fake flowers and vines wrapped along the rusty fence which separated them from the sidewalk. On sunny days they sat outside, and on cold days they huddled close to the heaters, counting the blue and white tiles scattered across the floor.

"You earned five-hundred bucks to pack out some utensils?"

"Yeah," she laughed. "I mean, every time I think I'm getting used to Victor and his antics, he comes up with something completely new. And at this point, I can't even try to come up with reasonable answers as to why he acts a certain way."

Hazel laughed and sat back on her chair, staring at the cars driving by.

"So this is my treat," Nika said. She checked her watch.

"Ouch, what happened to your wrist?"

"Oh, this?" Nika asked, staring at the scratch. "Roses nicked me today as I was replanting them. I had my gloves on, but they still caught my wrist."

"Ouch!"

"Gardening doesn't look that fun anymore, does it?" Nika laughed. "I swear the number of times I've gotten sunburned on my nose, scratched by a thorn, or gotten the heebie-jeebies from spiders. But the garden's looking great, and according to Sydney, they'll be getting tenants soon."

"So you're doing a great job, finally paying off your debts, covering your rent…"

"Exactly."

Hazel pointed at her. "Now, all we need to do is get Kurt to stop calling you. Sheesh! That guy is a borderline stalker."

Nika rolled her eyes. "Yes, well, he seems intent to keep reminding me of my 'biggest' mistake."

"I just wish we could do something about it," Hazel grumbled, pulling her lips in a thin line. "He changes numbers like it's nothing else. And those midnight calls—"

"Will stop tonight. I'm turning my phone off, and eventually, I'll get to changing numbers."

"Sounds like a good idea to me," Hazel grabbed her milkshake and took a sip. She smiled at the pink fluffy drink. "So, you ever think about Victor's company?"

"What do you mean?" Nika asked.

"Well, Strong Tech Vice, I looked it up a little, and it's all about Cyber Security, something that my boss is very much interested in," Hazel gave a small smile. "Maybe you can put in a good word for Gone Global for me?"

"So first of all, you want to meet Victor and flirt your way to riches? Now you want your boss to meet him?"

"The flirting part I wasn't too serious about," Hazel waved Nika off. "I did enough reading to tell that this isn't just some random guy you approach and talk to."

"I told you."

"But I might stand a promotion if Victor calls my boss," Hazel said. "You should've seen his face when he heard you were working as a gardener for him. He nearly fell off his chair!"
