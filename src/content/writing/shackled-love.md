"You're here," I cried.

"Nothing was going to stop me from finding my way to you," Dimitri whispered.

I could only laugh and cry at once. I clung to him tightly, taking in his scent, his grip, his hair. It reminded me of Monroe Castle. It reminded me of safety and freedom. And when the hug finally broke, I looked Dimitri in the eyes — and before I knew it, he leaned in, pushed a hand through my hair, and kissed me hard.

Whether it was the adrenaline, or the relief of seeing him, I didn't resist. I melted as new sensations of admiration and butterflies rose inside me. His other arm wrapped protectively around my back, and I was lost in the sweetest moment I'd had in a long while. I returned the embrace, wrapping both arms around his neck, returning the kiss.

Soon we had to pull back, but Dimitri kept me close to his side. I didn't mind — I was faint from exhaustion. We'd fought a heavy battle that day, but it had been a great victory. I watched the cries and cheers erupt alongside the flames surging from the tents.

"Ava!"

I turned to see Ryatte running toward me. Dimitri's grip tightened, but I shook my head.

He sighed, and let me go.

I ran to embrace Ryatte.

"So good to see you're alive."

"Heavens above, I'm blessed to see you in one piece too," Ryatte said. "I'd never have forgiven myself if anything had happened to you."

He let me go quickly, though — and I could feel Dimitri standing close at my side.
