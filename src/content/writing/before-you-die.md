I am haunted. Not by ghosts, oh no. I am haunted by the future memories of people when I make eye contact with them. In fact, just a singular prospective memory — I see the few mere seconds right before they will die. I never used to be bothered by them. These memories did no harm to my relationships, after all. Many of their memories consisted of them dying in the hospital, their hands wrinkled and cold in the far future. Others would die in the dark, alone, the rain pouring on their skin like icy darts.

I did not make mention to anyone of what I had seen. That was not the reason the sunglasses came up, nor the reason why I fled to the mountains. No. It was the reflection I saw in a young child's eyes. The haunting memory of seeing myself standing there, wielding the knife in my bare hands.

It wasn't any young child's green eyes I was staring at either. It was my daughter's.

Up into the mountains, I fled. I left my daughter safely in the custody of her father. Was I merely delaying the inevitable? Had fate cursed me to murder my one and only child? I could not and would not allow this to happen. I would rather die first. She never knew what I had seen either. So she probably believes that I had just abandoned her.

I made my way back home from the markets one week, following the cold grassy pathway leading to my house. I lived as a hermit, but I still had an internet connection, a warm fireplace, and great TV. After all, just because I was living a life of self-exile did not mean I was forbidden to have some form of luxury. I was just thinking of pouring myself a fancy glass of wine and helping myself to the latest movie when I spotted a vehicle in front of my house. I frowned, staring at the sleek, black and spotless car parked rigidly in a straight line, a meter from my wooden door. I walked up to see it open, a young man with a tidy suit and black sunglasses climbing out. He stared at me for a good long while, then lifted up a photo. As if he was comparing me to a woman in the picture. He lowered his sunglasses.

"Miss Longwood?"

"Yes," I whispered. "What do you want?" I was not one for politeness. I stood and watched as he approached me.

"We have been looking for you for a long time…"

"I'm afraid you'll be disappointed to know; I don't want to be found…"

"I'm afraid I haven't been given much of a choice. Can we talk inside?"

"No, and keep your sunglasses up; I don't want to look into your eyes," I said and crossed my arms.

"Yes, I have been made aware of your gift," the man spoke and smiled. I raised my brow. "My name is Jason. I work for the police department in North Wood."

"And why are you even here?" I asked. "No offense, Jason, but I would rather be left alone."

"I just have a few questions, if you don't mind."

"Actually, I do," I said and pushed past him, my steps crunching hard against the gravel. I just wanted this man gone.

"It's about your daughter…"

My hand froze on the knob, the metal suddenly melting into my hands as the memories of my past came rushing back. I gripped the handle tighter. "I don't want anything to do with her…"

"It seems like you may not have a choice, I'm afraid. When was the last time you were in contact with her?"

I turned around, staring coldly at the man before me. "Fifteen years," I responded before opening the door and rushing inside. I slammed it shut and peeked through the window. Jason did not seem flustered or frustrated. He merely walked up to the door and knocked.

"It's been a mighty long time, Miss Longwood, but I need you to understand that I would not be here unless absolutely necessary."

"Leave me alone!" I shouted and made my way to the tiny kitchen, pouring myself a glass of wine. I heard a second knock and a third, but I completely ignored it.

"I can leave you alone, Miss Longwood, but I can almost promise you your daughter won't… you're next on her list."

I sat silently as his words rang in my ears. What did he mean I was next? What was he referring to? I stood up again, slowly walking towards the door like a cautious cat.

"Your daughter, well, she's been up to some serious stuff, even as a teenager, got herself mixed up with the wrong crowd," Jason kept speaking. "Let's just say she's wanted."

"Wanted for what?"

"Are you going to let me in?"

"Wanted for what?!" I repeated. Jason's silence told me I wasn't going to like it. I opened the door, and he raised another sheet of paper. "Wanted for terrorism and murder."

I shook my head. "Now you're lying…"

"I wish I was, I sincerely do, but her father told me to come to you, said that you may have more answers… even though you weren't in contact… perhaps a memory?"

I slammed the door in his face and slid back down in horror and disbelief. This had to be some form of sick prank. Perhaps a setup of sorts. If my daughter was indeed anything like me or her father, then she wouldn't hurt a fly. This man was lying. I knew it.

"I know this will come as a shock."

"No, no, you're lying!"

"I sincerely wish I was, but your daughter is mixed up in some serious work, and we are desperate to find her…"

I slowly stood up, my hands trembling, pushing all thoughts of that hauntingly dark memory of mine. Was this how it would end? Would my daughter try to murder me, and I kill her in self-defense? Or could I perhaps prevent this fate that had been thrown into my lap? I had never tried changing destiny, but perhaps now, it just might be possible. I opened the door and gasped as I looked into Jason's eyes.

I saw him clutching his gun in a red sealed room, blood streaming from his stomach. I could hear the metallic footsteps, and a dark figured man stepped inside. "Hello, Jason… goodbye Jason…" Jason lifted his gun, but it was too late; the mysterious figure had fired his pistol, once in his chest and once in his head. He collapsed, everything growing blurry.

I swallowed hard as Jason raised his brow, "What did you see?"

I shook my head. "I'd rather not say…"

"Don't you believe even a small part of you could change someone's fate?"

"What happened to my daughter?" I asked, deliberately batting away his question. Jason sighed and shook his head.

"She got roped into some gang. At first, the ideas were a little far fetched, but they started to become more and more radical, and one can only assume she was brain-washed in staying."

I raised my brow and crossed my arms. "I bet you're just saying that to make me feel better."

"Look, all we know is that she is looking for you… we found you first, now we need your help."

I took a step back and realized what he was implying. He wanted to deliberately use me as bait to track down my daughter, whom I had not seen in fifteen years and had no intention of ever seeing again. He wanted her to find me. But what then? Does she attack me in the kitchen where I take the knife and stab her?

"No…"

"Miss Longwood…"

"No," I took another step back and slammed the door in Jason's face. Fates begone, I'm just going to avoid it instead.

I grabbed my travel bag and started stuffing some underwear and other clothing within arm's reach. I could hear Jason knocking on my door again and again. But this time, I ignored it. Nothing he could say would convince me to set the very trap that would end my daughter's life. I could not bring myself to believe she was a terrorist, and neither could I believe that she wanted to kill me. After all, why after so long? I knew I left her for fifteen years, but it just gave her reasons to completely cut me out of her life, not seek me out and destroy me.

I zipped up my last bag when I heard another knock on the door again.

"I am not leaving, Miss Longwood. You are in danger…"

I rolled my eyes. I was hardly the one in danger, but I was not going to tell him that. If he believed my daughter was such an evil criminal, heck, he would probably hand me the knife. I grabbed my car keys and the five other bags I had now packed and headed towards the backdoor. I unlocked it silently and placed everything in my rusty old car. I knew it worked. I checked it every second day. It was a getaway in case someone like him tried to track me down.

I climbed into the driver's seat and looked at the rotten apple core that was on the seat next to me. I guess it has been a while since I have driven. I started the engine and jumped as I heard a bang. I thought it was the vehicle but then realized it was coming from behind me. There was another bang and a fourth. With all my visions, I knew full well that those were gunshots.

I ducked down and heard shouts. They were coming from Jason. But I knew he would be ok. I mean, he died in a red room, there was nothing red in my cabin, and he should be fine… right?

I sighed, my heart pounding in my chest. Technically I wouldn't die until I murdered my daughter either, but that does not mean I would not get seriously injured. I did not want to spend the next couple of weeks in the hospital and decided that Jason is not a bad man. I pushed hard on the pedals and sped around the house. I could see that Jason's vehicle was out for the count, and he sat against the back, holding his gun, calling into his radio. I also looked up to see another black vehicle with two men shooting at the car. It seems they had not noticed me yet. I looked at Jason, who had now spotted me, and I motioned for him to come. He nodded, rose up, and fired a few rounds before bolting towards me. He sprang into the vehicle, and I sped off, down the gravelly road. Jason hit his head and grunted as he landed on the seat. He pushed his hand down and lifted up the apple core. I just smiled and shrugged, focusing on the road. The big problem with the attack was the fact that they pulled off the road. This meant it would take them a while to come after us, and I had no mercy on my car.

We sped down the lane and finally reached the highway, where I sped up even more.

"Give me a ticket later…"

"I believe you have a valid excuse… thanks for saving my hide…"

"You weren't gonna die there anyway…"

"Probably because in your heart of hearts you wouldn't have left me there."

I raised my brow, realizing that he was probably right. He was dead meat in that situation, yet as fate had handed to him, I was there with a vehicle ready to go. Bloody fate. I rolled my eyes and kept going. I frowned as I saw a motorcycle speeding past, the pink neon colors standing out as it zipped by.

"Um, Miss Longwood…"

"Chara…"

"Chara, I believe that was your daughter…"

I swallowed hard and gripped the wheel tighter, praying that my daughter had not recognized me. My heart pounded in my chest, louder than a set of drums at a rock concert. I looked in the rearview mirror to see her disappearing over the horizon. She was undoubtedly heading down to the cabin. It meant she knew where I was, and it told me she would have found me soon. I thought about the men that were there, but again, this was where fate was cruel in its own way. She would not die there… but I would make sure she would not die where she was destined to either.
