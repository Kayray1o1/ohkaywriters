"Shh," Emma motioned for Jackson to be quiet. She could hear it, creaking on the porch. She had stayed long enough in this old wooden house to know the different sounds. If it was a particularly windy day, the porch would groan under the pressure. It was a long elongated sound as if the house was stretching due to old age.

But there was now a distinctive sound she knew was not normal. The deep groan and creak, then another, in a distinctive pattern. It was subtle, but to her, it was footsteps. Someone was right outside their door, in the middle of the night.

Jackson stood straighter. He was leaning over to look at the map she was pointing out. However, his eyes were wide and alert.

A fire crackled from the chimney, and the creaking stopped. Emma brushed her hair out of the way as she slowly looked up at the window in front of her. She froze.

A figure stood in the dark. She could just make out the outline of his frame, and the whites of his eyes. He was staring at them, and he did not move as she saw him.

He wanted her to know he was looking at them. He wanted her to know that she was being watched.

Emma jumped as the door to the house rattled. She glanced at the figure again. He was still there. He was still frozen. She turned to Jackson who had reached for the poker stick that was leaning in the corner. He stretched out his arm towards her, as the rattling of the door became far more aggressive.

Jackson grabbed her arm and pulled her into the kitchen. Emma shut the door and locked it.

"You have your phone?" Emma asked, realizing that hers was still in the living room. She was not going to risk entering there again. Jackson struggled to pull his phone out of his pocket.

"You want to call the police?"

"What do you think?" Emma snapped as she reached for the knife drawer. "I might be FBI, but I am unarmed, my gun's in the safe… You think they're here to sell girl scout cookies?"

"Sorry," Jackson swallowed as he punched in the numbers on his phone. He turned to show Emma his screen.

She heard a deep crash of a door splintering to pieces.

It meant that those strangers stalking outside of her house were now inside. Emma slowly pushed the drawer closed, a sturdy carving knife now in her hand.

"911 what is your emergency?"

"Hello?" Jackson whispered. "Someone has broken into the house."

The kitchen door broke into another frenzied rattle. Emma and Jackson both staggered back.

"Are they inside? Sir?"

The door shattered open, but they were just met with complete darkness.

"Stay back! I've got a gun and we've called the cops!" Emma screamed out. It was completely silent. No one was saying a word. No one was storming in.

"We're in a cabin, 23rd Mural Street, it's on the outskirts of the city," Jackson whispered. "They're inside."

Emma and Jackson did not move as they watched the pitch-black darkness. The silence was worse than the noise. They knew someone was there. They were probably watching them. She wondered, was this some elaborate or sick prank? Or would these people have the audacity of attacking a federal agent right in her own home?

The time ticked away. She could swear she heard her heartbeat as well as Jackson's.

Then the light bulb flickered. Emma looked up at it and then at Jackson. He flipped his phone around just as the light went off. Leaving them in pitch darkness.

And the sound of someone running towards them caused Emma to take a sharp breath. She lunged forward with her knife, her knees shaking. However, the knife met its mark, sinking into the shoulder of the stranger in the house. He grunted and fell to the ground, her on top of him.

Jackson's torch on his phone finally lit up, revealing a masked man. His eyes wide and red, but he was not moving.

Emma jumped back, the knife still sunk deeply into the attacker's shoulder.

"Run!" Emma called out to Jackson. They both bolted past the attacker. Jackson was leading the way, firmly holding the metal poker stick in his hand and the torch in the other.

Emma took a deep breath of relief as sirens started ringing close by. They both stormed outside.

"Wasn't there another one?" Jackson asked turning in circles. It was freezing outside, with the bitter chill sinking into Emma's bones. Emma could barely think, the adrenaline pumping hard in her veins. She was not sure if she killed the guy in the kitchen, but Jackson was right.

There was more than one person out there. However, whoever he was was long gone by the time the cops arrived.
