Okay, I'm finding *way* too much joy watching a car catapult down a road, smacking into boxes and going flying. And what's worse? I didn't even realise that this — *just this* — took around two hours to learn.

Two hours.

What am I doing with my life?

So, I want to make a game. I have no experience, and I'll admit I've never touched code except that one time during the Covid lockdown when I wanted to master HTML because I had no life. It was the era where whole generations turned to the internet, since there was nowhere they could legally go.

I also wrote an entire book during that time — I like to call it my Covid Baby. It was the one thing from those years I was genuinely proud of. But I'm going off on a rant. My coding attempts back then fell very, very short.

Maybe it's because I had no real motive. It's the difference between learning a language for its own sake and learning it because you're travelling to the country, or have someone to speak it with. So two hours of game coding is already huge progress compared to what I managed then.

Still — why am I *this* excited about a car flying off the edge of a map and dive-bombing once it hits the end of the road? Even more so when I got it to collide with the boxes I lined up in front of it.

I think it's because I did something I never would've believed I could, even a month ago. It's so small. It's so simple. But it's a victory — worthy of being celebrated for ten years with eleven feasts a week!

Okay, maybe I'm exaggerating a little. But I'm excited, and that's fair. I don't know if it'll last — I'm still wrapping my head around the fact that this took me two hours. But the courses are designed so I actually *remember* everything, because there's no use finishing something successfully only to forget it the next time I open the software.

Memory is a fickle thing.

I think there's wisdom in taking the time to learn things properly. The value is in remembering, instead of wasting hours later trying to recall something simple. And second — find joy in the small victories. Life is miserable enough as it is; if we can't find joy in the small things, we'll be miserable 24/7.

And honestly, I just don't have the energy for that.

So I want to make a game. I also want to write about making a game. And I still believe I'm completely delusional about all of it.

Come be delusional with me!
