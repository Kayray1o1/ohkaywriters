Clary walked down the broken pathway in the dark. She knew her parents would have a fit if they knew what she was doing. But they were thousands of miles away — for once, far enough that she felt safe to do as she pleased. Not that she had any real plan. She'd just wander the dark shore for an hour or two, then go back to bed.

Perhaps she was running from the nightmares that had been haunting her. Perhaps the beach was simply calling her. Either would be her excuse if her aunt asked questions — not that her aunt was much for questions. Chances were she'd just shrug, hand Clary a cup of coffee, and keep watching her favourite programme on TV.

Clary gasped as her feet sank into the ground — her cue to take off her flip-flops. She bent down, fingers brushing against the coarse sand, and used her phone as a torch as she picked up her shoes and walked east. She edged closer and closer to the water, until the waves brushed over her feet and made her shiver.

The wind showed no mercy to her hair, whacking her face as she walked. Eventually she stopped.

"For crying out loud!" she said, trying to pull the hair out of her face.

"Wind's a bit rough today, isn't it?"

Clary jumped at the voice and noticed a guy sitting a little further uphill, his feet buried in the sand.

"Who are you?" she asked. She realised she sounded a touch high-pitched; her heartbeat had spiked. She hadn't planned on running into anyone this time of night.

The guy seemed to read her mind and raised his hands in surrender. "Relax, I'm just here to enjoy the view." He chuckled as if at a good joke. "Not that there's much to see anyhow."

"Well, it's dark."

"And there's that too, of course."

Clary took a step closer. He was young, much like her, in simple shorts and a blue shirt. His short blond hair waved around as frantically as hers, though he didn't seem bothered.

"So, mind telling me what a girl's doing wandering around in the middle of the night? I'm afraid I might get robbed," he said, smiling.

Clary smiled back, shaking her head at the absurdity. "I don't think thieves are out hunting tourists right about now."

"Of course not — or I'd hope not. This is a pretty private beach."

"Right." She realised he was probably a local. "I just came to clear my thoughts."

"Great minds think alike, then. The name's Lewis, by the way. Not that you asked."

"Clary."

Lewis nodded. "Nice to meet you, Clary. You sound new — are you from around here?"

"Sound?" Clary swung her light over him and noticed a white cane right beside him. "Oh, gosh, you're blind — oh gosh, I'm so sorry, that was so insensitive."

Lewis only laughed. "What? Never seen a blind man at the beach before?"

"No — yes, I mean —" Clary was completely flustered.

"Relax, I'm just messing with you," Lewis said. "And no, I'm not completely blind, if that's what you're wondering. But I'm impaired enough that I can't get around without this."

"I'm so, so sorry."

"Well, it wasn't your fault. Not unless you're responsible for the head injury I got as a six-year-old." He grinned. "You here for a while?"

"No, I moved here two years ago," he said. "But Blue Lagoon kind of traps you. Once you're here, there's really no going back."

"Well, I hope that's the same for me."

"Don't want to go home?"

Clary shook her head — then, remembering, gasped. "Oh, right, no…"

"You just shook your head, didn't you?"

"Guilty as charged."

"It's hilarious," Lewis said. "Now, I know you weren't really planning to, but you're more than welcome to join me."

"Sure, why not?" Clary stepped forward and sat down close to him. She noticed he had a whole picnic basket and a pillow to lean against. "You here for midnight snacks?"

"Who can go to the beach without a good snack on hand? It's tradition. Wait — you don't have snacks?"

"No…"

"The horror," Lewis muttered, so seriously that Clary had to laugh again.

"Well then, to keep good tradition, I'll share."

"Oh, no —"

"I insist. Besides, who can say no to good chocolate and peaches?"

"You brought chocolate and peaches?"

"Yes," Lewis nodded. "Chocolate for the health, and peaches for the enjoyment."
