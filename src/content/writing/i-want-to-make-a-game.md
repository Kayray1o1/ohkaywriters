It all started with a caffeine-induced stupor. I should seriously not be sitting in front of my computer when I come up with ideas — but unfortunately, I'm scared of my own thoughts even *without* a computer in front of me.

Okay. So I want to make a game.

Do I have qualified development experience? No.

Have I ever spent time designing in Blender? Well… no.

Do I know *anything* about gaming? I play 'em.

So I'm not exactly qualified. But I have a game idea. And information is free on the internet, so I can teach myself — right?

Right…

What on earth am I signing myself up for here? I keep hearing different things. It takes years, decades even, to master Blender. Making games is super difficult. It requires extensive problem-solving skills.

But I want to make a game…

I don't know what I'm signing up for. But neither did I when I chose to write a book. Has it been worth it — even despite the frustrations, the tears, the doubts? Yes.

I don't know much about game development. But I've already started a course, and I'm excited. Heck, I was thrilled to put a party hat on a monkey in Blender. Did I struggle to make my monkey blue and render it? Something that simple? Maybe. But the joy of pulling off a brief tutorial in software that looks hilariously complicated felt like clearing a massive hurdle.

And since I'm a writer, I thought — why not write about it? About my journey into making a game, the reasons I want to, and the struggles that come along for the ride.

I know for a fact it won't be easy. So I might as well take you all with me. If I'm going to suffer, you're suffering too.

So who am I? I'm a streamer, a writer, and I am delusional. But honestly, being delusional is the only reason I can sign up for anything and actually succeed. The troubles that come with succeeding are overwhelming at first — not impossible, but enough to scare people off if they look at the whole journey at once.

I know I'm going to be screaming at my computer. That's a guarantee at this point. If making a game is anything like editing videos, the screaming sessions are absolutely happening.

But I'm ready for it. I hope you are too. So tell me — what challenge do *you* want to take on? What do you want to learn?

And what have you been delusional about… and somehow succeeded at anyway?
