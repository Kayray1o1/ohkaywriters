When Lexi opened her eyes, she gasped. She sat upright and rubbed them. It was completely dark now. James was no longer in the kitchen, and her shoes sat neatly beside the table — she didn't remember taking them off. Frowning, she noticed a blanket draped over her and all the papers stacked tidily at her side.

She glanced at her phone. Three in the morning. There was no use going home now. She was a little worried about Alice, but she'd call to check on her later. Either way, she laid her head back against the pillow and blushed in embarrassment. She'd only meant to close her eyes for a couple of minutes.

She groaned and turned. This couch was possibly more comfortable than her own bed. She set her alarm for another hour — then she'd carry on translating the documents. It was the least she could do to repay James for accidentally sleeping over. She fell asleep, and it felt like only a blink before the alarm went off. She sat up, rubbed her eyes, and got to her feet.

"The light switch is somewhere…" Lexi felt along the walls. Logically, it should be near the front door. But as she patted around, she frowned.

So logic didn't work here. She kept feeling — until something struck her foot and she pitched forward, landing on the floor with a squeak. At least it was carpeted. Just as quickly, the light flicked on, and Lexi looked up. James stood in his bedroom doorway, hand on the switch.

"Of course, that's where they put it," Lexi laughed.

"You okay?" James walked over. She nodded, then shook her head as he offered a hand and helped her up.

"Just an idiot. I'm so sorry — first I fall asleep here, then I wake you up with a literal bang…"

"It's okay." James led her to the couch, and she sat. She stretched out her neck and looked at him. "I was hoping to carry on with the documents… that's why I was looking for the light." She glanced at the switch and shook her head. "You know, logic dictates switches should be by the front door."

"Yes." James stood and walked to the front door. He nudged a picture frame aside, revealing the switch. Lexi's eyes widened.

"Oh…"

A small laugh escaped him as he returned to his seat. "One of the apartment's more amazing details."

"How to keep thieves in the dark, 101," Lexi replied. She picked up the papers and sighed. "I'm so sorry…"
