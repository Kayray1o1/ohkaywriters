When the bell rang, Skye stood up and grabbed her bag before walking out. She took a long route back to her dorm, and the corridor was quiet. Everyone was either in classes, studying, or sleeping. She opened her door; the dusty window lit up the person sitting in the corner of the room. She shook her head.

"You know you make yourself look really dodgy sitting there in the corner."

A small smile formed on Aaron's lips. "Well, you never know who's lurking."

Skye closed the door and bolted it. Then, she checked her windows, closed the curtains, and switched on the lights.

"Heard from Slade you had an incident at the bank. What happened? Do we need to pull you?"

"No, I wasn't the one in trouble," Skye placed her hands on her hips, raising her brow as she looked at Aaron. He wore dark blue jeans, a brown T-shirt, and a leather jacket.

"The bank got robbed."

"What?"

"Shh," Skye motioned with her fingers. "The bank got robbed by that Caden Vexar thief."

"Once again, what?" Aaron repeated. "You should've pulled out immediately."

"Yeah, not so easy when you've got a gun pointed to your face," Skye pointed to herself. "I'm fine. Comparatively to everything else that has happened to me, that wasn't altogether the worst. I'm fine. I got the cash."

"So you're going to be ready for tomorrow?"

"Is that the final exam?" Skye asked.

"It works best with our schedules, and the AI security systems are updating, so Willow will be able to tamper with the feeds," Aaron explained. "You sure you're ok?"

"Look, I'm a little shaken. But…" Skye hesitated, shaking her head. "No one died. Not by his hands, anyway. An Enforcer caught someone else, though, for something. I wasn't too sure what and didn't want to stick around to find out."

"Good, at least we know you're capable of making smart decisions."

"Shut up. I wouldn't even be here if none of you weren't confident that I couldn't do the job."

Aaron nodded, and Skye sat down on her bed close to him. "You know, I'm looking forward to the Haven. If only for a quick Bible study."

Aaron nodded. "And a prayer session where we can actually speak out loud."

Skye rested her head against the wall, closing her eyes. "You ever think this is going to end?"

"Eventually," Aaron said. "But I don't think we'll be there to see it."

"What kind of life is this?" Skye stared at the curtains.

"A quirky one."

Skye started to laugh and shook her head. Aaron smiled.

"Well, you're not wrong," Skye said. "Which route are we taking this time?"

"Southeast, the tunnels."

"Risky," Skye whispered. "I don't like the tunnels."

"Me neither, but the Enforcers have been upping their patrols near the riverside. I think they're catching on that children are small enough to fit through."

"I wonder who snitched?" Skye looked up at the dark grey ceiling.

"I don't know, but it's been decided to take the tunnels."

Skye pushed her hands in her hair. The tunnels were notorious for the company they held. The worst kinds of people resided in them. Many were desperate. No one knew who they might encounter when they entered the tunnels.

By God's grace, they hadn't encountered the worst company. However, Skye hated putting the kids in greater danger than they were already. Escaping alone put them at risk of being killed, regardless of their age. And anyone caught helping them—well, the Tower existed for a particularly painful reason.
